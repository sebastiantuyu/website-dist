Tell me which experiments you’ve made, and I’ll tell you who you are.

It doesn't matter which kind of company do you have, whether a startup just starting or a big company that has 20 years in the market: making experiments around your business defines who your company is.

Why is this important?

Performing experiments constantly allow the business to check again if their hypothesis about the external world is still being valid or not. This is anything new, many startups have made very popular experiments from testing their MVP to launching new features.

Airbnb recently has been developing a new feature, where you can check internet speed from your host, this is one of my favorites because show a very interesting fact about Airbnb users, people maybe is traveling in a different way and now we may be traveling to work all around the world and for sure you need to have a good (or decent) internet connection.

Running in-house experiments

It’s important to find a balance between the time frame and costs related to the experiment, we don’t want to waste 10k just to figure out that our new feature is not good enough.
A very basic framework to experiment, with no-code tools is something like:

Facebook pages + Instagram + Forms + Landing page= feedback.

By itself, running an experiment is not enough but the feedback is. If we realised that our experiment attract a decent amount of people even without giving them the real product… we may find the X on the map.
